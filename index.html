Summary: “Machine learning (ML) is a type of artificial intelligence technology that relies on vast datasets for training. ML is currently being use in various systems for automated decision making.” [1] “The limitations of AI are generally perceived today thanks to the discourse on bias (…) ‘the employment of new technologies that reflect and reproduce existing inequalities but that are promoted and perceived as more objective or progressive than the discriminatory systems of a previous era.” [2] “A historical data set of successful applicants will essentially be a male-dominated data set,” “To give women equal opportunity in these areas, we need to look at how to de-bias the systems.” [3]

Purpose: Promote critical reflection on the impact of gender bias and how the development of artificial intelligence systems based on past social and cultural prejudices affects the society today. By increasing awareness of this issue, the aim is to encourage reconsideration of more ethical and inclusive technologies.

Objectives: Deconstruct gender bias by analyzing how social context influences machine learning and its impact on society.Identify relevant projects, debates, and quotes and relate them to social, algorithmic, and gender bias to promote discussion on the topic.Develop an interface that exposes the learning of algorithms based on past prejudices that promote gender discrimination, allowing for critical reflection on the machine learning process and its implications on society.

Methodology: Define, group, and relate relevant content such as projects, debates, quotes, and others, with social, algorithmic, and gender bias. Develop a facial recognition interface that highlights gender bias and promotes gender equality awareness. Create a diagram that presents the various layers of gender bias in facial recognition, as well as its relationship with social and algorithmic bias.

Expected Results: This project aims to stimulate reflection on the presence of bias in algorithms and promote the construction of more gender-equal algorithms, intending to contribute to the creation of a fair and equitable society.
